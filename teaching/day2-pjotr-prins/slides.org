* Take home messages

* FAIR Data?

- The FAIR Guiding Principles for scientific data management and stewardship
  Mark Wilkinson a.o., Nature Scientific Data (2016)
  20K citations
- Findable, Accessible, Interoperable and Reusable (FAIR)
- Is data on a USB drive in my pocket FAIR?
  + https://thebird.nl/data/Sample01/is-in-my-pocket

* Reproducible?

- Is FAIR data reproducible?
- NO - reusable is not reproducible

* Open data

- Not sharing is easier than sharing
- We still have silos and silos of unshared data
- Even UKBiobank hits the news - for unlicensed shared data
- If data is already in the open, why be fussy??

* Free and open source software (FOSS)

- Is FOSS software free software?
- NO - open does not mean free - it depends on the licenses and copyright
- Lots of open source software on the internet

* AI breaks the rules

- AI companies ignore copyright and licenses and slurp up every word on the internet
- This is harmful behaviour
- It is becoming a precedent

* Fait accompli

- Do away with copyright and licenses?
- Since we put our data, text and code on the internet anyway
- Where did copyright come from?
  - Copyright protects business interests
  - We adopted it to protect our code
  - Uniprot and wikipedia are getting ripped...

* AI is not FAIR

- Once the data is in a model it is not findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable
- Or is it?
- Some people think AI regurgitates
- There is randomness built in
- AI can make scary mistakes

* There are more concerns

The GNU Guix project decided to make a stand:

- title: Standing up for human crafting
=> https://codeberg.org/guix/guix-consensus-documents/pulls/13

Before we go over these, realise that I am a fan of AI power in both software development and the scientific effort!
The points here are by the Guix project and the value is that we need to address them.

* 1) The main AI companies are centralising power?

- Force you to run non-free software
- Data collection - using your phone, TV...
- Monitoring and sharing/selling
- Threat to privacy
- Potentially worse scenarios - what if?
- How can we change that?

* 2) AI ignores the law?

- Particularly copyright and copyleft
- Stealing all material
- With human help rewriting software so the license can be changed
- How can we change that?

* 3) AI demeans humanity

- The argument is that people become more lazy and stupid
- How can we change that?

* 4) AI is an ecological nightmare

- The current companies and investments generate huge emissions and material shortages
- How can we change that?

* Addressing these issues

- That is not going to be easy!
- We need local models that get produced by free software
- Improve models to be more efficient
- Improve models to be more reproducible
- I am convinced we can do it - though we may be looking into the abyss

* The abyss

- An immeasurably deep chasm, depth, or void.
- In the book of Genesis, the primeval Chaos out of which earth and sky were formed.
- The abode of evil spirits; hell.

So apt being next to Vesuvio

* Conclusion

- FAIR is not necessarily open data (but findable and accessible)
- FAIR data is not necessarily reproducible (but reusable)
- AI is not FAIR even though it is extremely useful - e.g. for creating ontologies and fine mapping QTL
- AI has its problems and we need to address them
